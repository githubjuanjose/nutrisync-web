NutriSync — Product Requirements Document (v1 / MVP)

Tagline: Hormonal care made easy — *sync your cycle, fuel your mind.* Document owner: Product (Lucia Cebrian, COO) Status: Draft for MVP build · Target ship: 2026 Q1–Q2 (per GTM roadmap)

1. Problem

Healthcare and mainstream nutrition were designed around male biology. Men run on a ~24-hour hormonal cycle; women run on a ~28-day cycle with four distinct phases (menstrual, follicular, ovulatory, luteal). "One-size-fits-all" diet and training advice ignores this, leaving women to self-experiment with food and exercise across phases — with the cost showing up as later diagnoses, worse outcomes, and frustration.

The opportunity: the FemTech market is projected to grow from ~US$10B (2018) toward ~US$70B by 2030 (Grand View Research). Existing apps (Flo, Clue, 28, flo-living) lead on *period tracking* but only partially cover science-backed cycle-sync nutrition, integrated fitness, AI personalization, and clinician-partnered distribution. That gap is NutriSync's wedge.

2. Vision

A tailored nutrition and hormonal-care companion that adapts daily food, training, and wellbeing guidance to where a woman is in her cycle — backed by clinical evidence, personalized by AI, and supported by a community.

3. Target users

Primary persona — "Olivia," 24–38, cycle-aware self-optimizer. Tracks her period already, exercises, cares about nutrition, and is tired of generic advice. Wants to know *what to eat and how to train today* given her current phase.

Secondary personas:

  • Women with cycle-related symptoms (PMS, low energy, bloating) seeking relief through nutrition.
  • B2B end-users: female employees reached through employer wellness programs (€35–€50 per employee/year is the B2B unit).

Validation to date: 120+ interviews, 100 women in early cohort, 96% cycle-aware, 86% engaged, 13K+ content views.

5. Scope — v1 / MVP

The MVP is the five screens validated in the pitch deck. Everything is mobile-first; the web app mirrors core flows.

5.1 Cycle Home ("Daily Guided Experience") — P0

  • Week strip (Mon–Sun) with today highlighted.
  • Cycle-phase ring showing current phase + cycle day (e.g., "You are in your luteal phase — Day 24").
  • "Edit period" to adjust cycle start/length.
  • Daily guidance card: what this phase means + 2–3 recommended actions (food focus, training focus, wellbeing tip).
  • Bottom tab navigation across the 5 sections.

5.2 Meal Plans — P0

  • Search recipes.
  • Featured + suggested recipes tuned to current phase.
  • Categories (Breakfast / Lunch / Dinner / Snack).
  • Popular recipes with kcal + prep time.
  • Recipe detail: ingredients, steps, macros, "why this fits your phase."

5.3 Community Forum ("Explore") — P1

  • Community trending posts.
  • Articles feed (sleep, energy, meal timing, etc.).
  • Search topics / articles / people.
  • Read + react; posting can be lightweight in MVP (comments/reactions), full UGC later.

5.4 Progress Tracking — P0

  • Weekly progress ring (e.g., "6 days").
  • Daily intake widgets: steps, water glasses.
  • Month calendar with logged days.
  • Per-meal logging (Breakfast / Lunch with kcal ranges).
  • Central "+" quick-log action.

5.5 Profile & Onboarding — P0

  • Onboarding: cycle data (last period, average cycle/period length), goals, dietary preferences/restrictions, activity level.
  • Profile: account, subscription status, privacy controls, data export/delete.

5.6 AI Personalization layer — P1 (foundational, partial in MVP)

  • Phase + profile → ranked meal and action recommendations.
  • "Apertus AI" partner branding appears in the deck; treat the recommender as a service boundary so the model can evolve. MVP can ship rules-based + lightweight ranking, then upgrade.

6. Non-goals (explicitly out of MVP)

  • Wearables integration (Apple/Google Health, Oura, WHOOP) — roadmap 2028+.
  • Menopause module — roadmap 2027.
  • Insurer / public-health program integrations.
  • Full social network / creator tooling.
  • Medical diagnosis or treatment. NutriSync is wellness guidance, not a medical device (see §8).

7. Cycle-sync content model (the core IP)

Each of the four phases maps to nutrition + training + wellbeing guidance. This is the structured content that powers Home, Meal Plans, and recommendations.

Content must be reviewed by the clinical advisor (Constanza Ramirez Milli) and labeled with evidence sources. Phase boundaries are personalized from each user's logged cycle data, not fixed.

8. Compliance, safety & trust

  • Positioning: wellness & education, not medical advice; no diagnosis/treatment claims. Surface a disclaimer and "consult a professional" prompts for health-flagged inputs.
  • GDPR / Swiss FADP: menstrual and health data are special-category personal data. Explicit consent, data minimization, purpose limitation, right to export/erasure, EU/CH data residency. (Switzerland + Spain are launch markets.)
  • Ethical data & privacy by design is a stated differentiator — make it a visible product feature (clear consent, no selling of cycle data, granular controls).
  • Content evidence-tagging and a clinical review workflow before publish.

9. Monetization (context for product gating)

  • B2C: Free tier + Premium at €5.99/month; in-app purchases (~€0.81/user/yr blended).
  • B2B: Employer wellness, €35–€50 per female employee/year.
  • Sponsored expert content.
  • Product implication: a clean free/premium feature gate and an org/seat model for B2B from the start (even if B2B UI is thin in MVP).

10. Release plan (aligned to GTM roadmap)

  1. MVP (2026 Q1–Q2): 5 core screens, rules-based recommendations, B2C free + premium, pilot-ready instrumentation.
  2. Launch (2026 Q3–Q4): beta pilots with employers/universities/clinics; referral ("Give 3, Get 1"); analytics-driven iteration.
  3. 2027: EU expansion, stronger B2B/seat management, menopause module, AI personalization upgrade.
  4. 2028: wearables integrations, US market entry.
